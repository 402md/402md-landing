## Design Context

### Users
Developers, protocol engineers, and infrastructure builders who care about how AI agents will transact on the web. They come from GitHub, Twitter/X, or direct links. Technically literate, design-aware, skeptical of hype. They've seen a hundred crypto landing pages and can spot AI slop instantly. Desktop-primary.

### Brand Personality
**Bold, precise, inevitable.**

402.md is not a side project or a personal blog. It's payment infrastructure for the agentic web — a protocol effort that treats the problem as already solved, now being built. The tone is confident without being arrogant. Think Stripe's composure: "this is how it works" not "this is why you should care."

### Aesthetic Direction
**Dark infrastructure brand.** Think: Vercel's black-on-black precision. Stripe's authoritative clarity. Coinbase's institutional confidence.

- **Theme**: Dark only. Near-black backgrounds, controlled light on dark surfaces. Not dark-with-neon-crypto — dark like serious infrastructure.
- **Typography**: Satoshi (display + body, varying weight) + JetBrains Mono (code/protocol snippets). Geist is an acceptable alternative. Bold geometric sans that reads as modern infrastructure, not editorial.
- **Palette**: Near-black base. Cool or neutral grays for secondary text. One accent color used sparingly — amber/gold (payment/money association) or sharp green (live/active). NOT purple (AI), NOT cyan (crypto), NOT blue (every SaaS). The accent is rare and earns its presence.
- **References**: Stripe.com, Vercel.com, Coinbase.com — the level of polish, confidence, and product-forward presentation.
- **Anti-references**: Warm cream "editorial" sites. Personal blogs. Crypto projects with neon and particles. Anything with "building in the evenings" energy. AI-generated landing pages with Bodoni Moda + Figtree, punchy fragment copy, and three-principles sections.

### Design Principles

1. **Protocol is the product.** Lead with what x402 does, not the story of how it came to be. Show the protocol working. Code > copy.

2. **Infrastructure confidence.** This isn't experimental or exploratory. The tone is "this is being built" not "I'm exploring what happens when." No hedging, no self-deprecation.

3. **Earned authority.** Don't claim importance — demonstrate it through clarity, precision, and polish. The quality of the presentation is the proof.

4. **Dark precision.** Every pixel is intentional. Dark backgrounds demand more attention to contrast, spacing, and hierarchy than light ones. Get it right.

5. **Innovation over convention.** 402.md is building infrastructure that doesn't exist yet. The design should feel like it's from the future, not from a template.
